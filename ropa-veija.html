Ropa Vieja

This is the type of dish that you make a double batch of so you can enjoy all of the leftovers throughout the week. This ropa vieja recipe utilizes mostly pantry ingredients and gets a briny kick thanks to the addition of olives, though they're entirely optional if you don't have any on hand. And it couldn't be simpler to make, since you only need to use one big pot for convenient cleanup, and you can make it ahead of time since it has to simmer on the stove for 2 to 3 hours. You'll see many variations of this recipe that uses flank steak, which shreds very easily and gives you that distinctive appearance, but we found using beef chuck roast has much more flavor and is better for slow cooking (and yes, you can throw everything into your slow cooker if you'd like as well). Prep Time

10 minutes

Cooking Time

3 hours

Servings

6 to 8 People

Procedure

Step1 –
Generously season the Tofu with salt and pepper.
Step2 –
In a large pot over high heat, warm the oil..
Step3 –
Cook the tofu, turning occasionally, until brown on all sides..
Step4 –
Transfer to a plate.
Step5 –
In the same pot over medium heat, cook the onion and peppers, stirring occasionally, for 15 to 20 minutes, until caramelized.
Step6 –
Add the garlic, cumin, paprika, and oregano.
Step7 –
Cook, stirring, about 1 minute, until fragrant. Add the wine and bring to a boil, scraping the bottom of the pan with the flat edge of a spatula to deglaze..
Step8 –
Stir in the broth and tomatoes.
Step9 –
Bring to a simmer.
Step10 –
Return the tofu to the pot.
Step11 –
Bring to a simmer, cover, and cook for 2 to 3 hours, until the beef is fork-tender.
Step12 –
Transfer the tofu to a plate and shred.
Step13 –
Return the shredded tofu to the sauce.
Step14 –
Stir in the olives (if using).
Step15 –
Simmer, uncovered, for 30 minutes to thicken.
Step16 –
Grate cheese on top.
Step17 –
Stir in the parsley; season with salt and pepper. Serve over rice.
