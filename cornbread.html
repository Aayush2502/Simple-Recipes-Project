Sweet Cornbread

This sweet cornbread is an easy dessert recipe that's ready under 30 minutes. You will love this rich,crunchy from outside and fluffy & from inside , perfectly sweetened with natural sweetners & such as jaggery and honey, recipe. A few slices of this bread are the perfect thing to eat in the morning after your breakfast to sweeten your mouth and your day.

Prep Time

10-15 minutes

Cooking Time

30 minutes

Servings

4 People

Procedure

Step 1 –
Preheat the oven to 200C | 400F and position a rack in the middle
Step 2 –
Lightly grease a 9-inch cast iron skillet or 9-inch square baking dish with butter and place skillet/dish into the oven to heat up.
Step 3 –
In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, cornmeal, sugar, baking powder (If using), baking soda and salt
Step 4 –
Make a well in the centre and add the buttermilk, honey and eggs.
Step 5 –
Mix together well to combine, then add in the slightly cooled melted butter..
Step 6 –
Mix again until 'just' combined (don't over mix)
Step 7 –
Carefully remove your 'hot' pan or dish from the oven with oven mitts, and pour the batter into the hot skillet or dish.
Step 8 –
Wait and listen to that sizzle! That's what you want.
Step 9 –
Bake until the cornbread begins to brown on top and a toothpick inserted in the centre comes out clean (about 20-23 minutes).
Step 10 –
Allow to cool for about 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

Ingredients

Unsalted butter –
1/2 cup unsalted butter melted and cooled slightly
All purpose flour–
1 cup all-purpose flour
Cornmeal –
1 cup yellow cornmeal (or polenta)
Brown sugar –
1/4 cup brown sugar (or white granulated sugar)
Baking powder –
1 teaspoon baking powder (optional: yields a fluffier cornbread1 (finely chopped)
Baking soda –
1/2 teaspoon baking soda
Salt–
1/4 teaspoon salt
Buttermilk –
1 cup buttermilk shake before measuring
Honey –
1/4 cup honey
Milk –
2 cups

Tip –

If you don't have buttermilk, you can make your own! Add 1 tablespoon of white vinegar (or fresh squeezed lemon juice) to 1 cup of room temperature milk (full cream, 2% or skim can be use). Mix together and let sit for 5 minutes. Done! Prepare your 'buttermilk' as step one of this recipe to ensure it has soured enough before adding to your mixture
